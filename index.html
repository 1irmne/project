Thai Silk

Ban Khok Muang silk is a handwoven textile that reflects the unique identity of the Ban Khok Muang community in Prasat District, Buriram Province. It preserves traditional wisdom in silk weaving and supports cultural tourism.

About Thai Silk

Khok Muang silk from Buriram Province is distinguished by the "Phak Kud" pattern inspired by Muang Tam Sanctuary. It is produced using traditional mud-dyeing techniques with clay from ancient barays, resulting in soft texture and long-lasting colors. The community also promotes handicrafts and cultural tourism rooted in Khmer civilization and local wisdom.

Silk Style

The weaving style and geometric patterns are characteristic of the Northeast region of Thailand. These textiles are particularly known for their intricate designs and the use of the ikat (tie-dye) technique applied to yarns before weaving begins.

Royal S Pattern

The S-pattern silk (Sirivannavari “S” motif) is a royal pattern designed to preserve Thai textile wisdom. The motif represents love, strong bonds, and the continuation of cultural heritage.

Important Timelines
